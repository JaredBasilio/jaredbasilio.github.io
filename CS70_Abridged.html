propositional logic

Symbol Name Description
\[\forall\] For All All of P is true.
\[\exists\] There Exists There exists a P which makes the statement true.
\[\neg\] negation Not P
\[\lor\] Logical Or Either P or Q is true
\[\land\] Logical And Both P andQ are true
\[\implies\] Implication If P then Q.

proofs

Proofs are essential in understanding properties of concepts in the course. Understanding them early on will help with understanding the actual notes.
Type of Proof Proof Description
Direct Proof Prove if the antecedent is true, then the precedent must be true. i.e. \[P \implies Q\]
Proof by Contradiction
Proof by Contraposition Prove if the precedent is false, then the antecedent must be false. i.e. \[\neg Q \implies \neg P\]
Proof by Cases
Inductive Proof This proof is usually used to prove for all ( \[\forall\] ) statements and commonly used to prove summation questions. Your proof should include these 3 properties:
  • Prove Base Case
  • State the inductive hypothesis
  • Prove the inductive step
Extra Notes/Observations:
  • the logical statements in the proof description are all logically equivalent.
  • pigeonhole principle: if we have k balls and n bins and k is greater than n, then atleast 1 bin should have more than 1 ball.
  • a | b reads "a divides b", meaning there exists an integer such that \[b = aq \]
    • if a | b and a | c, then a | (b+c)
    • if a | d and b | d, then ab | d
  • odd and even proofs can be simplified to utilizing 2k+1 and 2k respectively.

stable matching

The Stable Matching Algorithm is a pairing of two groups of n such that the resulting pair is happy. Each individual of each group will have a ranking of the other candidates of the other group from most preferred to least preferred.

Example of Ranking Table:
Candidates
A 3 > 1 > 2
B 1 > 3 > 2
C 3 > 2 > 1
Jobs
1 A > B > C
2 C > B > C
3 C > A > B
Stable Matching Procedure for a day
Morning All jobs will send an offer to the candidate on the top of their list
Afternoon Each candidate that received an offer will reject all offers except the one that is the highest on their respective list.
Evening Each job that just got rejected will cross off that candidate from the list.
Repeat this process until all candidates and jobs are paired
Observations from this algorithm:
  • Will always halt with a matching taking at most \[n^2\] days.
  • Improvement Lemma: the job on the candidates string can only get better while the candidate on the jobs string can only get worse.
  • Rogue Pairing: There exists a job and candidate that prefers each other over their current matching. This implies that matching is unstable.
  • The group that is offering is optimal for them. i.e Stable Matching is Job Optimal
